UNIVERSITY OF WATERLOO ALTERNATIVE FUELS TEAM


UWAFT (in short) participates in the 4-year long Ecocar challenge. The goal is to take a market car, gut it, and turn it into a hybrid. I joined at the end of Ecocar 3 and designed mounts for pumps and filters and also for go-pros for suspension filming.

In Ecocar 4, I did research to find suitable components for the car's interior. I calculated the torque, power, and battery capacity requirements to fulfill the cruise speed and regenerative breaking limit. I designed cases and mounts for important components such as the HV junction box and found ways to position it underneath the car. Unfortunately I have decided to take an option (sort of like a minor at UW) and have overloaded my course list and am no longer apart of the team.

Note: The CAD here is property of GM and was used by the team (myself included) to design components, such as the HV function box mentioned above. I worked with these large assemblies but did not create them (other than the small additions)

Ri3D (Robot in 3 Days)

Ri3D is a 72-hour building challenge for people who are no longer eligible to participate in FIRST. The nature of the challenge is to make a functioning robot the moment the challenge is announced by FIRST. This means that all the ideas are original and all the tasks need to be completed efficiently ideas are original.

I manufactured aluminum parts for the milk-crate carriage and assembled the elevator system on the photo to the right.

LINE FOLLOWING ROBOT


This robot was created during the lab of my Sensors and Instrumentation course. We had to design the op-amp circuits to filter for the correct data for the robot to work. I'm pretty proud of my soldering work since I managed to solder two surface mount 0805 resistors in series without shorting the pads (picture to the right). We needed a resistor that was 1.2 MΩ for the best gain and the lab's largest resistor was 1 MΩ. So instead of recalculating I wanted to challenge myself to solder two resistors on in series. Pretty much the reason I included this here.


GUITAR PLAYING ROBOT


For my final project of my first term of university, I worked with three other friends to create a lego robot that could translate guitar tabs from the internet and play them on a guitar.

I primarily did the mechanical side of the project and did a lot of the building but I also wrote code to make the frets play.

Fun fact, I learned how to play guitar because I thought it would be fun and might help with the design of the robot.
