INTERNSHIPS

KPM Power Inc


KPM power is a start-up located in Etobicoke, ON. They dedicate their business to creating internet connected lithium-ion battery fleets meant for forklifts. This allows management of the charging and replacement of battery cells to be efficient and simple to do.

During my intership at KPM Power, I pushed the progression state of their product. I led the other coops to create a local live data line that reads CAD messages and filters it to store in a SQL database which gets displayed on a monitor via PHP. I created many new battery CAD models designed around manufacturability (extrusion) and servicability while confined to the forklift battery standards (pictures available). I prototyped and improved PCB cases, an example is is background where everything but green pcb was CADed by me! I also did plently of soldering and created a lot of custom cables for customers and internal texting.

Miovision


Miovision is an IoT traffic company that pushes the boudaries of whats possible for the future of smart cities. The have two main product lines, the Scout and SmartSense. I worked on the older product, Scout, to update it's manufacturing process.

I created many GD&T drawings of CAD models for new more ergonomic and space efficient fixtures used to increase the production time of the cameras. I decreased the cost of producing cameras by fixing issues with the fixtures and decreased the production time by stream lining the assembly.

Environment and Climate Change Canada


Being part of the Cloud Research Division, I supported scientists develop their research tools that gather atmosphere data in the clouds. My most notable take away was the complexity of PCB design by optimizing size, traces, and minimizing the number of vias. I designed a PCB for counting particles, made aerospace cables, and created mounts for sunlight detectors.
